# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary users are hiring managers, technical leads, founders, and senior engineers evaluating Alejandro Ortiz Corro for a role, collaboration, or high-trust product work. They arrive with limited time and need a fast answer to three questions: can Alejandro understand business outcomes, can he design reliable software architecture, and can he execute with modern engineering standards.

Secondary users are potential clients in Mexico and international markets who need software, product systems, architecture review, or a serious implementation partner. They need confidence that the work will be clear, secure, scalable, maintainable, and tied to business goals rather than just a stack checklist.

## Product Purpose

The site is Alejandro's professional presentation and a public code sample for AOH&S. It exists to convert business and career interest into qualified conversations while showing engineering judgment through the site itself.

Success looks like:

- Recruiters and technical evaluators quickly understand Alejandro's seniority, product judgment, and business impact.
- Potential clients see enough proof to start a serious conversation.
- The public code demonstrates architecture, implementation quality, SEO, i18n, auth boundaries, media handling, observability, and deployment discipline.
- Private client and product code stays private while the site remains a transparent working sample.

## Brand Personality

Precise, architectural, outcome-driven.

The voice should feel direct, senior, technically confident, and commercially literate. It should avoid hype and avoid hiding behind stack names. The emotional target is trust: visitors should feel that Alejandro can turn ambiguous business goals into resilient product systems.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages with dark glow, glass cards, gradients, and fake metrics.
- Developer portfolios led by terminals, code snippets, stack badges, or "React portfolio" framing.
- Agency pages that overpromise with vague transformation language and no evidence.
- Open-source-community language that implies this is a collaborative OSS product.
- Beige editorial minimalism, magazine cosplay, repeated tiny uppercase eyebrows, numbered section scaffolding, and identical icon-card grids.
- Stack-first hero copy. Technology should appear as evidence, not the main promise.
- Overly decorative visual systems that make the site feel AI-generated instead of authored.

## Design Principles

- Outcomes before stack: Lead with business results, operating clarity, quality, security, and scalability; use technology as supporting evidence.
- Show the system: Make architecture, workflows, decisions, and tradeoffs visible enough that technical evaluators can assess judgment.
- Public sample, private work: Be explicit that this site is public source as a working example, while client and product code remains private.
- Evidence over claims: Use Casa Roca production proof, active product builds, enterprise experience, screenshots, architecture notes, and resume content to support every major claim.
- One domain, clear boundaries: Keep public SEO pages and private dashboard on `aohys.com`, but make the private surface authenticated, noindex, and operational.
- Accessible polish is credibility: If the site claims quality, the typography, contrast, i18n, resume parsing, privacy, and reduced-motion behavior must prove it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for public pages and dashboard UI. Body text contrast should comfortably exceed minimums. The site must support reduced motion, keyboard navigation, visible focus, semantic landmarks, readable line lengths, and no color-only meaning.

The public site is bilingual: English is the default, Spanish lives under `/es/`. Language choices must be explicit and stable for SEO, not browser-detected redirects. The resume PDF must be ATS-friendly and readable by humans: single-column, text-based, clear section labels, and no decorative structure that harms parsing.
